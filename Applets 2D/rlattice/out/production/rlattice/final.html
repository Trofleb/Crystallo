The Complete Reciprocal Lattice

If both are activated, we can see below a superposition of the original lattice and the corresponding reciprocal lattice. The basis vectors of the original lattice are a and b respectively whereas the basis vectors of the reciprocal lattice  are a* and b*. Each reciprocal lattice vector h can be expressed by a linear combination of the basis vectors h = ha*  + kb* where h and k are integer. The direct and reciprocal lattice's vectors are mathematically related  by the following scalar products of vectors :

a a* = 1 a b* = 0 b a* = 0 b b* = 1

In this application, we have limited ourselves in the two-dimensional plane. However, in order to study the atomic  structures of real crystals by diffraction, it is necessary to  extend both lattices (direct and reciprocal) in 3D. Consequently, to the three basis vectors defining the unit cell of the crystal structure a, b and c correspond the three reciprocal lattice vectors a*, b* and c*. The angles  between the direct lattice vectors are α, β and γ whereas the corresponding angles between the reciprocal lattice vectors are α*, β* and γ*. In three dimensions, the reciprocal lattice vectors are expressed by the following scalar products :

a a* = b b* = c c* = 1 a b* = a c* = b a* = b c* = c a* = c b* = 0
